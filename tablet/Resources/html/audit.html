Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test (AUDIT and AUDIT-C)

History and guide

AUDIT:

  • Originals: Fleming et al. (1991), www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1743817; Saunders et al. (1993), www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8329970, and others.

AUDIT-C:

  • The AUDIT-C is a three-item derivative version based on consumption questions from the AUDIT (the first 3). There are several phrasings:
  • Bush K, Kivlahan DR, McDonell MB, Fihn SD, Bradley KA (1998). The AUDIT alcohol consumption questions (AUDIT-C): an effective brief screening test for problem drinking. Ambulatory Care Quality Improvement Project (ACQUIP). Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test. Archives of Internal Medicine 158: 1789–1795. http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9738608
  • Bradkey KA, Bush KR, Epler AJ, Dobie DJ, Davis TM, Sporleder JL, Maynard C, Burman ML, Kivlahan DR (2003). Two brief alcohol-screening tests from the Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test (AUDIT): validation in a female Veterans Affairs patient population. Archives of Internal Medicine 14: 821–829. http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12695273
  • http://www.integration.samhsa.gov/images/res/tool_auditc.pdf
