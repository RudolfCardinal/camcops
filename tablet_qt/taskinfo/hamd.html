Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (HDRS) / Hamilton Rating Scale for Depression (HRSD, HAM-D)

History and guide

See Hamilton (1960), www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14399272, and NINDS CDE on HAM-D, www.commondataelements.ninds.nih.gov/CRFs/Hamilton%20Depression%20Rating%20Scale%20(HDRS).pdf?CrfId=F0807.

Also Hamilton (1967), www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6080235, and Williams (1988) for the SIGH-D, the structured interview (www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3395203).

The first 17 items constitute the HDRS-17. The last 4 items are intended to subtype the depression, not rate its severity.

Scoring consensus: from www.cnsforum.com/clinicalresources/ratingscales/ratingpsychiatry/depression/

The 7-item abbreviated version is by McIntyre et al. (2005), www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16301700.
